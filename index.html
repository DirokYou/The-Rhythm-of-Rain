The Rhythm of Rain

Dirok Yousif - 27 March 2026

I absolutely love rain.

It's hard to say exactly what there is about rain that I love so much. But I have a couple of worthy points.

The Dome Effect

Listening to rain feels like sitting in a private dome. It feels like it covers my entire body, including my eyes and ears. In my case, it's a feeling only attainable through listening to the sound of rain. Not pink noise or brown noise, but the sheer sound of rain.

I could sit completely alone, doors and windows shut within my room, yet not feel the same effect as what rain gives. The dome effect is only activated once the sound of rain is turned on. And it doesn't matter where I am seated. I could be on the bus, in the gym, or in my room. The same effect is achieved.

This might in fact be the sole reason why rain calms me down. As a very introverted person, I find myself in need of privacy. A space where I can calm myself down, let go of constant thinking, and recharge.

Sitting in my room doesn't recharge me. It might even make it worse for me. My mind drifts easily, and when there's nothing to do, it drowns in thoughts. But rain blocks that. This effect is especially effective for people who struggle with calming down and overthinks a lot.

A Temporary Different World

The sounds of rain share similarities with brown noise and probably pink noise too. But rain is natural and irregular. It could be either, neither, or both at the same time. But what stands out the most for me is where the sound takes me mentally.

I have three main places that I like to think of during rain. Yours could be different.

The first place is at a castle. I imagine a stone castle out in a green European forest. There is a lot of moss growing on the castle, and it's always set on higher ground.

The second place is inside the forest. I imagine a walkable path walled with high trees poured with rain. I picture sitting inside a big wooden house, on the porch, watching water flow over the leaves.

My third favorite place is inside a big city, somewhere like Stockholm or Gothenburg. I'm either sitting high up in an apartment, looking down on the city with the window open. Or somewhere on the ground, close to the people. Watching the cars drive by in the pouring water.

The most beautiful part about this is that these places are possible in real life. It's not fantasy, it's real-life scenarios.

Rain in Real Life

Listening to rain is one thing, but experiencing it IRL is almost twice the effect. However the conditions are higher.

I have to sit in a very relaxed spot where I can lower my focus. There should be no stressing factors waiting to be handled.

I could sit high up in an apartment with the window open, or lay on a swing bed out in the country looking into the forest.

Experiencing rain IRL has the same effect as listening through my phone. But you also feel the rain, and you see the rain, and you can even smell the earthy scent of rain pouring on the ground.

Rain can be freezing cold, but during spring and summer is the peak feeling of rain. It's not cold, it's soothing. And it trickles down on your skin.

“Some people feel the rain, others just get wet” is a quote that I will pass on to my kids. I'll teach them how to enjoy rain, play in it, or just appreciate the beauty of it.

After all, every single life depends on it.

The Rhythm of Rain

Rain has a special rhythm, different from rhythm like music. It does not have a pattern or tempo, no swing or sway to it. Rain just falls. It's constant and random.

Friends question me for listening to rain during a hard workout. But I recommend it for multiple reasons. One of them being, like mentioned, it's constant. There is no timing, buildup, or drop to it. It's just there, pouring down on you. And if I crave pink noise, I'll listen to light rain. If I'm feeling brown noise, adding thunder is the go-to.

As an overthinker, the constant noise helps me not to think. That doesn't work with music. Because music makes me aware of the rhythm. Rain doesn't.

And that's the differene.

Reflection on Rain

Rain is more than just water falling from the sky; for me it creates silence, imagination and reflection. Whether I am listening through my headphones or sitting by an open window, rain creates a world where the mind can finally rest. And maybe that's why it was created the way it is, not only to give life to earth, but also peace to those who listen.
